Material Selction

Selecting the right material for this project is crucial. So for start, we start by listing the things the item is going to face.

Requirements Expectations Limitations
  • Cheap
  • Impact resistance
  • Easily accessible
    		•  Strong enought to be used over 30 times without fracture. Easy to replace
    does not damage the rivet upong riveting
    Does not interfer with riveting

    We have decided to use the material onyx since it's cheap, easily accessible and can be 3d printed and infused with carbon.

    Prototype 1

    This is the first prototype printed using onyx, it is able to survive over 5 rivets before crack occur on the cap.
    A second cap was printed with carbon fiber re-enforced, a visable dimple can be seen after riveting.

    All test was performed on the same rivet gun, anvil and test piece to ensure a middle.

    We have concluded that the part require more modifications to ensure the cover is reliable to be used in the long term.
    The Total cost to print out a single cover is 20c and 25c for carbon infused.

    Prototype 2

    As requested by some teachers, notches are added to help with removal without the need of a new tool.
    Modifications were made to ensure deformation does not occur easily.

    Test shows sligh deformation after extended use without rest.

    Further testing shows no other deformation nor cracks (20rivets). Final test reviews that the product is able to survive <=50 rivets.

    Carbon infused

    We tested carbon infused rivet covers to see if it would improve the quality of the cover.
    This in-turn made the cover really hard making it deform and crack easily.
    In conclusion, infusing carbon would worsen and increase the cost of the cover.
