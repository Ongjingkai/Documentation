What is an Composite

A composite material is a material which is produced from two or more constituent materials. These constituent materials have notably dissimilar chemical or physical properties and are merged to create a material with properties unlike the individual elements. Another term for composite structures is also known as Fibre Reinforced Plastics or FRP

How it's produced

  • Fibres are manufactured by first creating individual filaments
  • Filaments are then assembled into tows (160000 filaments per tow)
  • Fibrous tapes – produced directly from bundles of tows running in one direction and held by laterally small cross-threads

Wrap Orientation

  • Strength of a fiber is greatest when loads are applied in the running direction of the fibers (i.e. 0º warp position)
  • However, aircraft composite structures are designed to carry multi-directional loads, i.e. the fabric layers are laid at different angles to each other with reference to the 0º warp position to form a laminate (reinforced by the resin matrix)
  • Hence, fibrous tapes are unidirectional, while the fabrics are bi- or multi-directional

Wrap Orientation 2

  • Anisotropic stress design – warp fibres are laid only in one direction, e.g. unidirectional fibrous tapes
  • Quasi-isotropic stress design – capable of carrying loads in more than one direction but not all direction, e.g. when the warp fibres are laid perpendicular to each other (i.e. 0º and 90º)
  • Isotropic stress design – capable of carrying loads in “all” directions. Warp fibres are laid in orientations of 0º, 45º, 90º and -45º
  • In repairing composite laminates, the repair material must follow the original structure’s warp orientations
