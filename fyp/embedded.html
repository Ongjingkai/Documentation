Software and printer used

The program used in this demonstration is eiger, and the 3d printed used during my FYP is Mark-Two.

Embeddeding setup

Embedding for 3d printing

To summarize it, embedding for 3d printing is just start to a print, pause it midway, embed components into the 3D print job, and then resume the print, allowing it to 3D print over the components you have embedded.

Step 1

Import your STL file into your library

Step 2

Set your setting for the part you imported.

Step 3

Under settings, turn off supports. Eiger does not support support blocker unlike cura, take this into consideration when design your part to be printing using this machine

Step 4

Click on internal view.

Step 5

Ensure the support does not show up in the internal view.

Step 6

Select the layer you would like the print to pause to allow you parts to be embedded.

The option to pause after layer will only appear AFTER you select 2D.

Step 7

Save the part and print

For more help about embedding click here

Printing

Now that you are done with setting things up, this is how to embedded the nut into the print.

Step 1

Start the print

Step 2

The part would pause after on the layer you set to pause, remove the printing bed and insert the nut. Accoding to the manufacturer, the machine has only have a shift of 10μm.

Step 3

Repeat what you did for the smaller nut.

Step 4

Allow the printer to finish printing.
