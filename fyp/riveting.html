What is riveting

Riveting is a forging process that may be used to join parts together by way of a metal part called a rivet. The rivet acts to join the parts through adjacent surfaces. A straight metal piece is connected through the parts.

Type of rivets

There are 2 type of solid shank rivet used in the workshop, one of which is the flathead countersunk rivet(Left photo)
the other rivet used is the universal head rivets(Right photo)

Rivet diameter

When riveting ensure you know the type of rivet you would be using and the apporate diameter the end of the diameter should form into.

Steps

Step 1

Put some tape over the area you intend to rivet and mark out the area you intend to drill on the tape.

Step 2

To aid you in drilling, Use a dot punch to mark the area you intend to drill

Step 3

Align your drill 90 degrees to the work piece and drill. Countersink the area if you are using flathead rivets.

Step 4

Ensure you use the correct anvil before riveting.For a thin metal sheet, you can do the backing and riveting alone.
